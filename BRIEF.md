BRIEF_A
ONE VERB: STAMP
GAME IN 10 WORDS: A royal seal-bearer stamps moving documents before they crumble
WHAT THE PLAYER MASTERS: Reading document drift speed and pre-positioning the stamp for perfect ink contact — not chasing, predicting
THE WOW MOMENT: On wave 4, a forged document appears identical to a valid one — the only tell is a tiny watermark revealed for 0.8 seconds before it slides under the pile. Stamping it wrong brands you a traitor and reverses all your score.
WHY RICARDO: Metal Slug's instant threat-reading — you scan a document like you scan an enemy sprite, extracting one critical signal in a fraction of a second. Gran Turismo's pre-positioning discipline — the stamp must land before the document reaches the corner, so you're always setting up two moves ahead.
EMOTIONAL ARC:
  start: One slow document drifts by. You press. Ink blooms. It feels satisfying and weighty.
  groove: Three documents moving at different speeds — you're dancing, leading each one, stamp landing with rhythmic thwacks.
  climax: Six overlapping documents, two forgeries in the stack, the pile shifting as each stamp alters the order beneath. You are reading chaos.
WHAT MAKES IT HARD: Documents partially overlap — stamping one repositions the stack, changing the trajectory of the next. Your actions create the problem you have to solve.
SKILL CEILING: A 1x player reacts to each document individually. A 10x player reads the entire stack's collapse order, stamps bottom-to-top to control the cascade, never lets a forgery surface.
VISUAL: #C8A96E (aged parchment background), #1A3A5C (imperial ink blue), #D64E2A (forgery red stamp) — top-down flat lay, camera slightly tilted as if leaning over a desk. Reference: Papers Please meets Zuma's tactile satisfaction.
AUDIO: 92 BPM, dry bureaucratic woodblock rhythm with a low wax-seal thunk on perfect stamps — signature sound: a deep hollow CLONK when a forgery is caught, like a gavel
NOT: No sorting puzzle with infinite time. No match-3 chain mechanic.
GAME NAME HINT: seal-of-office
